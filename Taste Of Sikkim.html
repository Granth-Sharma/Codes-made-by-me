Most Famous Foods of Sikkim

Momos - There is no doubt that Momos also known as dumplings are the most popular delicacy among the people of Sikkim. Momos have become a popular snack in restaurants and street stalls across the country, not just in Sikkim.

Thukpa - A tasty noodle soup with origins in Tibet's eastern regions, this Sikkim cuisine is sure to please your palate. Almost every restaurant in the state, not just the street kiosks, serves this wonderful dish.

Kinema Curry - Kinema curry is a popular Sikkimese dish best served with rice. When dried in the sun, Soybeans impart a distinct flavour to the curry and are a popular snack among Sikkimese. It's high in protein and is seasoned with various flavours before being mixed with fermented soybeans. Some vegetarians use it as a meat replacement. The curry's distinct flavour is due to the fermentation of soybeans.

Some native beverages of sikkim

Jaanr - Jaanr is a native Sikkim beverage produced from barley, cassava root, wheat, and maise. It is available in various flavours at local shops known as Makai ko Jaanr, Jahun ko Jaanr, Gahun ko Jaanr, Bhaate Jaanr, Simal tarul ko Jaanr, etc.

Tongba - Tongba is a millet based alcoholic beverage and it is the traditional and indigenous drink of the tribal people of Sikkim the Limboo. Tongba is culturally and religiously important to the Limboo people. Offering tongba is a symbol of respect to a guest among the Limboo people and lso an important drink for special occasions and festivals.

Chhaang - The chhaang or chang is brewed from rice and it is an alcoholic beverage very popular among the locals of Sikkim. It is used in traditional ways in festivals of Tibetian and Bhutia people of Sikkim.

Some famous dessert of Sikkim

Sel Roti - This sweet dish is actually a popular street food item in Sikkim. It is sweet bread made from ground rice, banana, and sugar. It's like India's answer to the American Pretzel!

Dhan ki Kheer - it is made from the local rice (dhan). this rice is short grained and slightly plumped. A creamy desert love my the people of sikkim
